+_01_+

Overview

This qualitative research study examined how independent musicians navigate the process of booking live shows — from identifying venues and reaching out to promoters, to managing logistics and building ongoing relationships.

Independent musicians operate without the infrastructure of labels or booking agencies, relying on personal networks, digital platforms, and self-taught knowledge to secure performance opportunities.

+_02_+

Research Question

How do independent musicians identify, pursue, and manage live show bookings without institutional support — and what tools, strategies, and relationships enable or hinder that process?

+_03_+

Methodology

Media Elicitation was used as the primary data collection technique, prompting participants to bring and discuss screenshots, messages, and other artifacts tied to their booking experiences.

Journey Mapping synthesized participant data into an end-to-end map of the booking process, surfacing where friction and informal workarounds most commonly occurred.

  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Media Elicitation
  • Journey Mapping
  • Purposive sampling
+_04_+

Key Findings

Booking was consistently described as informal and relationship-driven, with personal connections to venue staff serving as the primary pathway to securing shows.

Social media functioned as both a discovery and credibility tool, while cold outreach remained a significant pain point with low response rates and no standardized process.
