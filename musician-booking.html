Qualitative Research Study

How Independent Musicians Book Shows

Qualitative research exploring the booking process for independent musicians.

Overview

This qualitative research study examined how independent musicians navigate the process of booking live shows — from identifying venues and reaching out to promoters, to managing logistics and building ongoing relationships with venues.

Independent musicians operate without the infrastructure of labels or booking agencies, relying heavily on personal networks, digital platforms, and self-taught knowledge to secure performance opportunities. This study set out to understand their workflows, pain points, and strategies in depth.

Research Question

How do independent musicians identify, pursue, and manage live show bookings without institutional support — and what tools, strategies, and relationships enable or hinder that process?

Methodology

The study used a qualitative approach centered on in-depth interviews with independent musicians. Participants were recruited across genres and career stages to capture a range of experiences with the booking process.

Data was analyzed using Thematic Analysis to surface recurring patterns, tensions, and strategies across participants. Themes were developed inductively from the interview transcripts.

  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Thematic Analysis
  • Purposive sampling across genre and career stage

Key Findings

Analysis surfaced several core themes around how independent musicians approach the booking process, including the central role of personal relationships with venue staff, the challenge of cold outreach, and the use of social media as a primary discovery and credibility-building tool.

Participants consistently described booking as an informal, relationship-driven process with limited standardization — pointing to a gap between how musicians experience booking and the tools currently available to support it.
